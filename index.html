Social media encourage toxic over-consumption and beauty standards

Social media platforms are designed to keep users engaged and scrolling, which can lead to a constant bombardment of new products and trends.

Modern social media platforms such as Tiktok and Instagram highlight the phenomena of rapid trend rotations in makeup and skincare, leaving little time before something new becomes popular.

The emphasis on expensive beauty products within the online beauty community can perpetuate issues such as classism and overspending. It can create a culture of exclusivity and financial strain for those who feel pressure to keep up with the latest trends. It can also promote harmful beauty standards and the idea that only expensive products can provide effective results.

These prices and rapid trend cycles place a burden on all industry consumers, largely women, which encourages unnecessary overspending on products that have been criticized for furthering harmful beauty standards and targeting female insecurities for corporate profit.

Be aware of your consumption.
